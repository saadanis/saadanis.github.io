Let's talk about what makes a good flag. I think that a good flag should be easily recognizable, be fairly simple, and not have any of the ugly colors. And these rules are subject to discretion, because they're mine.

But if you do wish to know, the NAVA (North American Vexillological Association) thinks that a good flag should be:

  • simple,
  • meaningful,
  • using 2 to 3 basic colors,
  • not using letters or seals, and
  • distinctive or related.
I don't follow all of these rules, and neither does NAVA.

First, the greatest flag in the world: Amsterdam.

The three Xs are Saint Andrew crosses or saltires. Like Roman Mars said, this is the absolute most bad-ass flag in the world. It looks amazing, it's got the best color (black), the best letter (X), it's simple, yet powerful and striking, and as an added bonus, it's actually based on their city coat of arms. I applaud the Netherlands for that.
