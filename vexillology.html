Vexillology is the study of flags. Though some vexillographers and vexillophiles love the history of these flags, I do not. Although I love the idea of seeing a battle ensign on a warship or the Jolly Roger on a dark skeletal ship in the fog, I love the design of these flags more. Sure, it helps to know about the history of these flags to appreciate them even more, but it sometimes stains the flag with the dark and awful deeds that were done under its shadow.

Some of the best looking flags have awful history. The infamous swastika of Nazi Germany, the hammer and sickle of the Soviet Union, the saltire of the Confederate, the sun of the Imperial Japanese Army, and the ensign of North Korea, are some of those examples. The most obvious reason I can think of is that these flags were made to be intimidating and that they weren't designed by committee.

I love flags only for their design. This is why flags with the Union Jack on the canton usually get a lower rating in my head, although they have history with Great Britain. They just look ugly. Tons of stars make flags unattractive too, no matter how many states you have got. Flags with their seal slapped in the center get no affection from me either.

What makes a flag good? The best place to get a basic understanding of that is North American Vexillological Association's "Good Flag, Bad Flag" booklet. They have got five principles which they do not exactly follow themselves.

  1. Keep it simple enough that a child could draw it from memory.
  2. Symbolize something from its history or culture or something of the sort.
  3. Use only 2 or 3 basic colors. They should contrast well.
  4. Don't use text or seals on the flag.
  5. Keep it distinctive or related.

If there's just one thing you wish to keep in mind, remember this:

Flags flap and drape, twist and turn, and are in most occasions far from you. You must be able to identify it in every circumstance for it to be a good flag.

Because I love flags so much, I must talk about some of my favorite flags. Therefore, I present the greatest flag in the world.

The Flag of Amsterdam

This flag is a beauty. Three stripes, two colors and the three saltires in the fess. As Roman Mars said, this is the absolute most bad-ass city flag in the world. But I say that it's the most bad-ass current flag in the entire world. It looks quite intimidating having the best color (black) and the best letter (X). It's simple, yet powerful and striking, and as an added bonus, it's actually based on their city coat of arms. I applaud the Netherlands for that.

Now, my top five. I do have a few state and city flags that I like, but I do not want to go into that rabbit hole, since I've already fallen into the national flag one. So, top five current national flags in no order but the alphabetical:

Albania's flag is strikingly beautiful with its black double-headed eagle in the center. It looks menacing with its red background.
Canada has red too, but the white makes it a place of peace. The maple leaf, simple and symbolic, atop a white square makes the flag lovable and inviting.
The tricolor flag of Estonia is simple. There's no symbol or star, yet it is one of the most beautiful national flags. The black in the center contrasts perfectly with the white in the bottom and the blue in the top. Simplicity is quite pretty.
The next flag is Iceland. It has the famous Nordic cross that is shared between Iceland, Norway, Denmark, Finland, and Sweden. The white separation between the red and blue makes it a lot better than if it were only red and blue. Although the design isn't unique, the fact that it shares it with its Nordic sisters creates a beautiful relationship between the countries.
The fifth flag is the legendary Union Jack. Made by the merging of the red St. George's Cross on the flag of England, the white St. Andrew's Cross on the flag of Scotland, and the red St. Patrick's cross of Ireland, it is most iconic flag in all of the world. This flag speaks royalty and great ships. It makes me want to wear a monocle, sip tea, and sing God Save the Queen.

It's so bad that it's crossed the line and come around to look like a masterpiece.

These are the top-notch in my eyes. I think they look the coolest, have the cleanest designs, or are just pretty unique. Most of them also follow the NAVA guidelines.

B+ and B

These flags are second-tier. They are good flags, with good design and aesthetic, but lose their A due to a bad color choice and increased complexity.

C+ and C

These flags are either not good enough to get a B, but good enough to above D+. They generally look good, but their complexity loses their grades.

D+ and D

These flags are not entirely awful. Their seals and very complex emblems lose their grades. Their color makes them unappealing, and they could be become better with some effort.
